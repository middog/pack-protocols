## The Three Stages

### ðŸŸ£ PROJECT â€” The Experiment

**Energy:** Individual or small team
**Risk:** Low (if it fails, it's a learning)
**Coordination:** Minimal
**Duration:** Bounded

**Sounds like:**
- "I want to try making..."
- "Let's see if this works..."
- "My small team could prototype..."

**What projects need:**

- Minimal bureaucracy
- Access to basic resources
- Permission (explicit or implicit)

**Success looks like:**
- Something exists that didn't before
- At least one person cared enough to finish
- Learning happened (even if outcome was "no")

### ðŸŸ¢ PARTY â€” The Coordinated Event

## Not Everything Should Grow

Critical insight: **Growth is not always good.**

**Some things should stay projects:**
- One-time experiments
- Personal expressions
- Things that lose magic at scale

**Some things should stay parties:**
- Annual traditions
- Community rituals
- Things that work at current scale

**Questions before growing:**
- Does this *need* to be bigger?
- Who's asking for growth? (them or you?)
- What gets lost at the next stage?
- Is there capacity for the next stage?

---

## Regression Is Okay

Practices can scale back to parties.
Parties can scale back to projects.
This isn't failureâ€”it's adaptation.

**Signs it's time to scale down:**
- Energy depleted, not renewed
- Going through motions
- Resentment instead of enthusiasm
- "We have to" instead of "We want to"

**How to scale down gracefully:**
- Name it explicitly ("We're simplifying")
- Celebrate what was, don't mourn
- Give permission to let go
- Preserve the seed for future growth
