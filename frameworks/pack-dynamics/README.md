## Sensing Before Herding

Dogs don't manageâ€”they **sense and respond**.

Before intervening, ask:

### Scent Trails
- Where does work actually flow? (vs. where the org chart says)
- Who do people actually go to for answers?
- What are the informal communication channels?

### Pack Position
- Who's leading? Who thinks they're leading?
- Where are the tensions between formal and informal authority?
- Who's the "alpha" in practice vs. title?

### Energy Patterns
- Where's the energy? Where's the drain?
- What activities generate momentum vs. consume it?
- When do people light up? When do they shut down?

### Territory
- What does each group "own"?
- Where are the boundaries unclear or contested?
- What happens when territories overlap?

## Reading Resistance

When the pack pushes back:

| Resistance Type | What It Signals | Response |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| Silence | Fear or disengagement | Safety first. Ask, don't tell. |
| Cynicism | Past betrayals | Acknowledge history. Small promises, kept. |
| Busy-ness | Avoidance or overload | Reduce before adding. |
| "We tried that" | Pattern recognition | Ask what was different then. |
| Enthusiasm | Buy-in OR appeasement | Test with small commitments. |

## Pack Archetypes

Common roles you'll encounter:

### The Alpha (Formal)
Positional leader. May or may not have actual influence.
**Engage:** Respect position while sensing actual authority.

### The Alpha (Informal)
Who people actually follow.
**Engage:** Critical ally. Get them early or get resistance.

### The Scout
First to try new things. Takes risks.
**Engage:** Perfect for pilots. Give them room to explore.

### The Guardian
Protects the pack from threats (real and perceived).
**Engage:** Address their fears directly. They're often right.

### The Connector
Knows everyone. Moves information across silos.
**Engage:** Essential for understanding actual flows.

### The Skeptic
Questions everything. Often labeled "difficult."
**Engage:** Often the best diagnostic. Ask what they see.

### The Quiet Carrier
Does the actual work. Rarely credited.
**Engage:** Find them. Acknowledge them. They know everything.

## Diagnostic Questions

Use these in discovery:

1. "Walk me through how [X] actually gets done."
2. "Who would you go to if you needed [Y]?"
3. "What's the thing everyone knows but nobody says?"
4. "What's working that we should protect?"
5. "If you could change one thing tomorrow, what would it be?"
6. "What have you tried before? What happened?"
