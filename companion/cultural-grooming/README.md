## Burnout Prevention

Change work is exhausting. The Companion watches for burnout:

### Early Warning Signs

| Sign | What to Watch |
|------|---------------|
| **Cynicism increase** | Negative comments, disengagement |
| **Reduced effectiveness** | Same effort, worse outcomes |
| **Exhaustion** | Physical, emotional, cognitive fatigue |
| **Withdrawal** | Less participation, more silence |
| **Irritability** | Shorter temper, less patience |

### Prevention Strategies

- **Protect recovery time** â€” Transformation can't be sprint forever
- **Celebrate milestones** â€” Acknowledge progress, not just completion
- **Rotate heavy lifting** â€” Don't exhaust the same people
- **Model sustainable pace** â€” Leaders work reasonable hours visibly
- **Check in genuinely** â€” "How are you?" with space to answer honestly

## Psychological Safety

Google's Project Aristotle found psychological safety is the #1 predictor of team success.

**Psychological safety means:**
- Safe to take risks
- Safe to ask questions
- Safe to admit mistakes
- Safe to offer ideas
- Safe to challenge

**Building safety:**
- Leader admits own mistakes first
- Thank people for raising concerns
- No punishment for honest failure
- Curiosity before judgment
- "What can we learn?" not "Who screwed up?"

## Facilitation Practices

### Retrospectives

Regular reflection builds improvement muscle:

**Format:**
1. What went well? (Celebrate)
2. What could improve? (Learn)
3. What will we try? (Commit)

**Cadence:** Every 2-4 weeks, non-negotiable

### One-on-Ones

*~sitting with one pack member at a time~*

- Weekly or bi-weekly
- Their agenda, not yours
- Listen more than talk
- Ask about blockers
- Ask about feelings, not just tasks

### Team Agreements

Explicit norms reduce friction:

- How do we communicate?
- How do we handle conflict?
- What are core hours?
- How do we handle mistakes?
- What's our definition of done?

## Conflict Navigation

Conflict is healthy if handled well:

### Conflict Stages

1. **Latent** â€” Conditions exist but not surfaced
2. **Perceived** â€” Parties aware of tension
3. **Felt** â€” Emotional involvement
4. **Manifest** â€” Visible conflict behavior
5. **Aftermath** â€” Resolved or suppressed

### Healthy Conflict Practices

- Name it early (before it festers)
- Focus on interests, not positions
- Separate people from problems
- Seek to understand before being understood
- Look for win-win, not win-lose
